This source document describes the Krebs cycle, also known as the citric acid cycle or TCA cycle. The document explains this as a series of chemical reactions occurring in the [[mitochondria|mitochondria]] that oxidize acetyl-CoA to produce CO2 and electron carriers (NADH and FADH2) for the electron transport chain. The cycle is named after Hans Krebs, who characterized it in 1937.

Key points:
- Alternative names: citric acid cycle, TCA cycle
- Location: [[mitochondria|mitochondria]]
- Function: oxidizes acetyl-CoA to CO2
- Produces NADH and FADH2 for the electron transport chain
- Second stage of [[cellular-respiration|cellular respiration]]
- Discovered by Hans Krebs in 1937
